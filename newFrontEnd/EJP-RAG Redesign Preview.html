What's the standard for good-time credit on a Class 2 felony in Illinois, and how does day-for-day calculation work?
⚖️

Under Illinois law, good-conduct credit for most Class 2 felonies is awarded on a day-for-day basis — one day of credit for each day served — pursuant to 730 ILCS 5/3-6-3. The credit applies to the sentence imposed, not the maximum statutory term.

Certain offenses are excluded from day-for-day eligibility and instead earn credit at a reduced rate (typically 4.5 days per month) under 730 ILCS 5/3-6-3(a)(2). These exclusions include first-degree murder, aggravated criminal sexual assault, and several specifically enumerated felonies.

The Illinois Department of Corrections has discretion to revoke earned good-time credit for disciplinary infractions; see 20 Ill. Adm. Code 107 for the procedural requirements.
